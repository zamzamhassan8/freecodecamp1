Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and “father of the Green Revolution.”

He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his work in developing high-yield crops.

Here's a timeline of his life:

  • 1914 – Born in Cresco, Iowa.
  • 1933 – Attended University of Minnesota.
  • 1944 – Led initiatives in Mexico with modern agricultural production.
  • 1965–70 – Helped Pakistan and India double wheat yields.
  • 2009 – Passed away at age 95.

He also spoke out on the promise of genetically modified crops as long-term solutions.
